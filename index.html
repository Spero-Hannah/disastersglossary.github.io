Disasters Research Glossary

On this page you will find disaster research-related terminology and associated definitions.

Terminology Definition Usage in Literature
Community-Based Interventions (CBIs) An open-source and community-driven toolkit provided to communities to advance their resilience; based on the principles of participatory action research.
  • Pfefferbaum R. L., Pfefferbaum, B., Van Horn, R. L., Neas, B. R., & Houston, J. B. (2013). Building community resilience to disasters through a community-based intervention: CART applications. Journal of emergency management (Weston, Mass.), 11(2), 151-159.
  • Niekerk, D. V., Nemakonde, L. D., Kruger, L., & Forbes-Genade, K. (2018). Community-based disaster risk management. In Handbook of disaster research (pp. 411-429). Springer, Cham.
Community-Based Research (CBR) Work and research that actively engages with communities to find and deliver solutions to landslide risk together.
  • Anderson, M. G., & Holcombe, E. (2013). Community-based landslide risk reduction: managing disasters in small steps. World Bank Publications.
Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR) Research must be based on a community-academic partnership; must have a methodology including or informed by direct interactions with the community, often occurring in homes or meeting places of the community.
  • Lichtveld, M., Covert, H., El-Dahr, J., Grimsley, L. F., Cohn, R., Watson, C. H., ... & Kennedy, S. (2020). A community-based participatory research approach to Hurricane Katrina: when disasters, environmental health threats, and disparities collide. American journal of public health, 110(10), 1485-1489.
Community-Based Disaster Preparedness (CBD Preparedness) Preparedness must Involve placing a specific value on the knowledge and capacities of local people and building on local resources, including social capital.
  • Allen, K. M. (2006). Community‐based disaster preparedness and climate adaptation: local capacity‐building in the Philippines. Disasters, 30(1), 81-101.
Community-Based Disaster Prevention (CBD Prevention) For application, CBD Prevention is an essential component of sustainable development in societies. Placing value on preventing disasters from affecting certain communities, but does not necessarily require direct engagement with said communities.
  • Nakamura, H., Umeki, H., & Kato, T. (2017). Importance of communication and knowledge of disasters in community-based disaster-prevention meetings. Safety Science, 99, 235-243.
  • Pandey, B. H., & Okazaki, K. (2005). Community-based disaster management: empowering communities to cope with disaster risks. Regional Development Dialogue, 26(2), 52.
Community-Based Disaster Management (CBDM) Management practice must include community participation - communities' contribution to the disaster management cycle can be started from the basic steps of a process and end in the community's achievement and institutionalization.
  • Buckland, J., & Rahman, M. (1999). Community‐based disaster management during the 1997 Red River Flood in Canada. Disasters, 23(2), 174-191.
  • Jahangiri, K., Izadkhah, Y. O., & Tabibi, S. J. (2011). A comparative study on community‐based disaster management in selected countries and designing a model for Iran. Disaster Prevention and Management: An International Journal.
Community-Based Disaster Risk Reduction (CBDRR) CBDRR education is defined as an approach that seeks to: (1) reduce the vulnerabilities and increase the capacities of vulnerable groups and communities to cope with, prevent, or minimize loss and damage to life, property, and the environment, (2) minimize human suffering, and (3) hasten recovery.
  • Sakurai, A., & Ito, T. (2022). Community-Based Disaster Risk Reduction Education in Japan. In Interlocal Adaptations to Climate Change in East and Southeast Asia (pp. 89-99). Springer, Cham.
  • Shaw, R. (Ed.). (2012). Community-based disaster risk reduction. Emerald Group Publishing.
Community-Based Leadership in Disaster Resilience (CBLDR) Interplay of community leadership and partnership with external development actors, with a specific focus on including vulnerable groups (such as women).
  • Abenir, M. A. D., Manzanero, L. I. O., & Bollettino, V. (2022). Community-based leadership in disaster resilience: The case of the small island community in Hagonoy, Bulacan, Philippines. International Journal Of Disaster Risk Reduction, 102797. <\li>
  • Hosseini, S. H., Amanat, N., Ghanbari, V., Nakhaee, M., Abbasabadi, M., Najafi, M., ... & Pashaei Sabet, F. (2017). Community-based management challenges in disaster risk reduction: a content analysis in Iran. Health in Emergencies and Disasters, 2(2), 63-70. <\li>
  • Ridzuan, A. A., Kadir, M. J. H., Yaacob, S., Oktari, R. S., Zainol, N. A. M., & Zain, M. M. (2017, July). Community resilience elements and community preparedness at Bukit Antarabangsa. In AIP Conference Proceedings (Vol. 1857, No. 1, p. 110003). AIP Publishing LLC. <\li> <\ul>
